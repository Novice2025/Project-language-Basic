üìñ LEARNING THROUGH STORYTELLING

Why Stories Work for Brazilian Learners:

  • Context over grammar: You remember patterns by seeing them in action
  • Natural flow: Stories force you to use past tenses correctly
  • Emotional memory: You remember "we were driving" better than "past continuous rule"
  • Multiple patterns: One story teaches 10+ grammar points simultaneously
  • Speaking practice: Retelling stories = real conversation practice

üìù STORY METHOD: Step-by-Step

  1. Read model story (Scotland restaurant example)
  2. Identify all past tenses (was/were/stopped/looked)
  3. Notice pattern: WAS/WERE + -ing for scene-setting
  4. Find time expressions (last week, one evening, when)
  5. Rewrite story with YOUR experience
  6. Record yourself telling it (audio)
  7. Listen and count errors
  8. Correct and re-record

üéØ PRACTICE ASSIGNMENTS

  • Week 1: Tell a story about last weekend (5 sentences)
  • Week 2: Describe a strange experience (10 sentences)
  • Week 3: Explain your last vacation (15 sentences)
  • Week 4: Tell a professional story (job interview/meeting)
  • Assessment: 3-minute recorded story with less than 5 errors
